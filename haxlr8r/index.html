Product Description

An  ecosystem of easy-to-use, open devices and software for interfacing with the physical world. Modular devices for controlling LED arrays, christmas lights, motorized displays, and more. Standardized software for developing amazing light shows and theater experiences. Also included is a web service for showing and sharing projects, displays, and lighting sequences.

Status of Product Development

For the last year, we’ve been developing various hardware modules and software packages for interactive theater experiences, and we would like to use Haxlr8r as a platform to refine our designs, bringing the modules into a unified ecosystem, retooling them for manufacture, and standardizing our software interfaces.

Our Team’s Ability to Attract Users

Matt has several years experience as a community manager at Make Magazine, a major DIY Tech magazine and blog. We are both members of NYC Resistor, a major hackerspace with a large public outreach in New York City. Additionally, we’ve been working the past several months to bring our interactive lighting projects to wider audiences at national music festivals and local events. Most recently, we collaborated with a group of people to put on an interactive light show for a performance and party with over 600 attendees.

Why Should You Choose Blinkiverse?

We have a solid vision of how we want the Blinkiverse ecosystem to work on both the high level and the technical level. We would like to use our experience at Haxlr8r to refine our designs and get them ready for manufacture. We have experience and a strong understanding of how to make electronic hardware, and we would like to take our skills to the next level and learn how to make our designs manufacturable on a large scale.
